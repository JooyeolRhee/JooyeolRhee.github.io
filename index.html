01 Research

What we work on

AXIS advances mixed-signal integrated circuits for ultra-low-power, high-accuracy sensor interfaces, power management and energy harvesting, data converters, and edge-AI / in-sensor computing. Our mission rests on four axes: accuracy that holds over time and temperature, system-level energy efficiency, compact integration, and robustness in real-world deployment.

Theme · PMIC / EH 01

Power Management & Energy Harvesting ICs

Our PMIC research enables battery-constrained or battery-free operation in distributed sensors and edge devices. We study autonomous start-up, adaptive power-tracking algorithms, and multi-mode DC–DC conversion with integrated protection and safety. We also develop on-chip regulation using low-dropout regulators (LDOs) and precision references (bandgap references, BGRs), and investigate power delivery for AI servers and HBM stacks.

FocusStart-up for energy-scarce sources · maximum-power-tracking control · mode-transition efficiency · on-chip regulation (LDOs) · precision references (BGRs) · AI-server / HBM power delivery.

Theme · AFE 02

Precision Analog & Sensor Interfaces

We design low-power, low-drift analog front-ends for environmental, industrial, and biomedical sensing. Emphasis is placed on accuracy over temperature and lifetime, input stages tailored to each sensor's source characteristics, and robustness to interference.

FocusPrecision amplification · source-matched readout · calibration and system-level linearity.

Theme · ADC / DAC 03

Data Converters (ADC / DAC)

We develop energy-efficient data converters tailored to sensing and instrumentation. Architectures are chosen per application to achieve high effective resolution at low supply voltages, and are co-optimized with analog front-ends and digital post-processing.

FocusConverter-system co-design · dynamic-range management · background calibration · verification from behavioral models to silicon.

Theme · ISC / Edge 04

In-Sensor Computing & Edge AI

We explore near-sensor analog pre-processing and low-overhead feature extraction to reduce data movement and energy. Designs are co-optimized with the sensing modality and the downstream digital pipeline.

FocusAnalog-domain preprocessing · event-driven capture · standardized interfaces for photonic, resistive, and capacitive sensors.
